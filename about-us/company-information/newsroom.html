Tata Starbucks Leadership Changes

Mumbai, 17 December 2015: In October, we celebrated the third anniversary of Tata-Starbucks. We are encouraged by how our customers in India have embraced the unique Starbucks Experience in this strategic market with great enthusiasm. Please find attached a letter which Avani Davda, ceo, Tata-Starbucks shared today with the company’s 1,400 partners (employees) in India announcing her decision to pursue opportunities outside of the company. The Tata-Starbucks Board has deep appreciation for what she has done to firmly position Starbucks as the premier coffeehouse in India. In her letter, Avani also announced that Sumi Ghosh has been appointed by the Tata-Starbucks Board as the new ceo of Tata-Starbucks effective January 1, 2016. An eight-year Starbucks partner, he most recently served as the vice president of Starbucks Midwestern U.S. region. Sumi brings three decades of consumer retail experience, including 10 years in brand management and operational leadership roles.

Avani and Sumi will work closely together through early February during this transition. As Starbucks fastest-growing new market, we are taking a long-term view in India and believe there is a tremendous opportunity ahead of us. We wish Avani great success in her future endeavors and welcome Sumi to the Tata-Starbucks family.
